When to Surf in Morocco — Seasonal Surf Conditions Guide

Timing is everything. Learn when to chase massive winter swells, when to find uncrowded perfection, and the best months for beginners to surf the Moroccan coastline.

Timing is everything. Learn when to chase massive winter swells, when to find uncrowded perfection, and the best months for beginners to surf the Moroccan coastline.

Morocco boasts an incredibly long coastline exposed to the power of the North Atlantic Ocean. While you can technically find ridable waves 365 days a year, the experience shifts dramatically depending on the season. Whether you are hunting double-overhead barrels or mellow peeling waves to practice your pop-up, you need to time your trip right.

Prime Season: Winter (October to March)

This is when Morocco truly shines. Deep active low-pressure systems in the North Atlantic send long-period groundswells marching down to the African coast. During these months, the legendary point breaks like Anchor Point, Safi, and Boilers wake up. The winds are often light offshore in the mornings, providing glassy, perfect conditions. While the water temp drops to around 16–18°C (61–64°F) requiring a 3/2mm or 4/3mm wetsuit, the air stays pleasantly warm.

Shoulder Season: Spring and Autumn (April-May & September)

The transition months are highly underrated. The massive winter swells become less frequent, but the water is warmer and the crowds thin out significantly. You will find fun, chest-high waves that are incredibly rippable and forgiving.

Summer: The Beginner's Dream (June to August)

Summer sees smaller swells and heavier onshore winds originating from the north (the 'Taros' wind). While advanced surfers might find it frustrating, summer is absolutely pristine for total beginners. Spots like Magic Bay in Imsouane and the sheltered beaches around Essaouira provide friendly, knee-to-waist-high whitewater that is perfect for learning the basics under the hot Moroccan sun.

Summary

Timing is everything. Learn when to chase massive winter swells, when to find uncrowded perfection, and the best months for beginners to surf the Moroccan coastline.

Key Takeaways

  • Winter (Oct-March) offers world-class groundswells
  • Summer is small, warm, and ideal for absolute beginners
  • Shoulder seasons offer the best balance of weather and quiet lineups
  • A 3/2mm wetsuit is perfect for winter months
  • Summer water temps allow for spring suits or boardshorts

Why It Matters

Arriving in the wrong season can ruin a surf trip. Advanced surfers need winter swells, while beginners will progress much faster in the gentle summer conditions.
