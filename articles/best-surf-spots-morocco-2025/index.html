Best Surf Spots in Morocco 2025 | Complete Surf Guide

A complete guide to Morocco's top surf breaks, from Taghazout's legendary point breaks to the raw Atlantic power of Essaouira.

A complete guide to Morocco's top surf breaks, from Taghazout's legendary point breaks to the raw Atlantic power of Essaouira.

Morocco has quietly become one of the world's premier surf destinations. Stretching along the Atlantic coast, the country offers an extraordinary variety of waves — powerful beach breaks, long mellow points, and heavy reef slabs. Taghazout remains the crown jewel: a small fishing village north of Agadir that hosts waves like Anchor Point, Killer Point, and Hash Point. Each works best on different swells and tides, offering variety for surfers of all levels. Further north, Imsouane boasts the longest right-hand wave in Africa. Down south toward Mirleft, explorers find uncrowded gems. The surf season runs year-round, with the peak Atlantic swells arriving October through March.
